Designated car parking areas, Brickmakers creek and open space make up for the Woodward Park existing built form and facilties. The current experience in Woodward Park can be described as over-scaled, disconnected and lacking diversity.

New proposed facilities on stage 1 of the Master Plan are focused on the west side of Woodward Place along the T-way and include a brand new regional leisure and aquatic centre that sits on the site of a restored creek corridor that maximises Woodward Placeâ€™s open space flexibility by providing regional sports and playground facilities. The Memorial Avenue Community Centre, the Netball NSW and the NSW SES Liverpool unit will be relocated and there is the option to retain the north-west car parking facilities for improved accessibility to the creek corridor.
Stage 2 aims to repurpose the existing north-east and south-west car parking facilities in order to develop two main catalysts for Woodward Place. A multi-purpose indoor and outdoor sporting facilities will be integrated in the Leisure and Aquatic Centre completed during stage 1. A new regional market hall will be located in the north-east corner of Woodward Place at the intersection between Memorial Avenue and the Hume Highway.
During stage 3 facilities on the north edge of Woodward Place will be relocated, particularly Girl Guides, Meals on Wheels and Liverpool City Pipe Band. The existing Whitlam Leisure Centre building will be repurposed for a community event space and facilities, while its pools will be removed from the site. These existing buildings will be refurbished to allocate community facilities, which will also be located in new community buildings in the north sector of Woodward Place.
The KU Marcia Burgess will be relocated and the eastern sports field will be relocated to the new sports park facilities. The Whitlam Leisure Centre northern car park will be permanently removed during this Master Plan stage. A new K-12 school will accommodate for the Autism Advisory & Support Service as well as shared community facilties. An exhibition and event centre will further connect the different catalysts of Woodward Place, fronting the siteâ€™s civic core and aligning with the market hall along Hume Highway.
